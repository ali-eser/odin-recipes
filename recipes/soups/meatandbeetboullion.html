Meat-and-Beet Boullion

Active cooking time: 30 mins | Total cooking time: 2 hours | Serves 8-10

Prompto would be happy to learn that this dish, commonly referred to as borscht, is very popular in Eastern Europe.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb stew beef, cut into chunks and rinsed (450g)
  • 9 cups cold water (2L)
  • 1 tbsp salt (7.5g)
  • 6.5 oz cooked beets (~4 small-medium beets) (180g)
  • 1 tbsp red wine vinegar (15mL)
  • 1 tbsp white sugar (12g)
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste (30mL)
  • 1⁄2 large onion, finely diced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (15mL)
  • 2 carrots, finely grated
  • 6 small golden potatoes, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 11⁄2 cup shredded cabbage (133.5g)
  • 7 oz (~half a can) diced tomatoes (200g)
  • 1⁄4 cup chopped fresh parsley (5g)
  • 1 tsp chopped dill (.5g)
  • 1⁄4 tsp pepper (.5g)
  • 2 cloves of garlic, cracked and pressed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • dollop of sour cream and additional dill for garnish

How to

  1. Put beef in large pot with 9 cups (2L) of cold water and salt. Bring to a boil, skimming foam off the surface. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer 50 minutes, regularly skimming more foam.
  2. Finely chop or use food processor to “puree” beets. Mix with vinegar, sugar, and tomato paste in a bowl. Set aside.
  3. Sauté onion with olive oil for 2 minutes, then add grated carrots and sauté another 3 minutes or until soft.
  4. After the meat has simmered 50 minutes, add potatoes and simmer for 10 minutes. Add beets, cabbage, onions, carrots, and diced tomatoes. Cook another 10 minutes. Stir in parsley, pepper, and garlic. Add bay leaves and salt to taste, then cover and remove pot from heat. Let stand 20 minutes. Remove bay leaves and garlic and garnish with sour cream and dill.

Effect(s)

Strength +100, HP +500
